Capitalization

Follow the capitalization guidelines in The Chicago Manual of Style. Some guidelines are specific to our documentation:

  • See the Jitterbit Terminology Glossary for the correct capitalization of Jitterbit terms.
  • Don't use capitalization for emphasis.

The types of capitalization styles referenced are as follows:

Titles and Heading

  • Use title case. For example, Component Dependencies, Deletion, and Removal.
  • Don't capitalize articles (a, an, the), coordinating conjunctions (and, but, or, nor), the "to" in an infinitive, and prepositions (with, to, for, in, from) unless they start a sentence.
  • For hyphenated words, capitalize the first element and the subsequent elements unless they are articles, prepositions, and coordinating conjunctions.
